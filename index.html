My name is Joshua Helton and I am a junior at Mississippi State University in Computer Engineering. This is my dissection project for Embedded Systems (ECE 4724) at Mississippi State during Spring of 2025.

The device that I chose is the Llano Laptop Fan. I chose this product because of my interest in motor and led control, as well as the circuit design of a product that I constanly use.

2. Market Analysis

Product History

The Llano V10 is a high-performance laptop cooling pad designed to enhance thermal management for gaming and high-performance laptops. It features a powerful 4.72-inch external turbofan, adjustable stand, RGB lighting, and a 4-port USB hub. The V10 is suitable for laptops ranging from 13 to 17.3 inches in size.

Product Operation

Human-Machine Interfaces

×

The Llano laptop fan contains a variety of input/output devices to provide the user with a convenient experience:

  • (In) 5x SPST switches
  • (Out) 1x four digit seven segment display
  • (Out) 2x USB-A port
  • (In) 1x USB-C port
  • (Out) 1x USB-C port
  • (Out) 1x Fan motor
  • (Out) 1x LED strip

The devices contains 5 input SPST switches. They specifically are the LED mode switch, LED color switch, power on/off switch, fan motor decrement speed switch, and fan motor increment speed switch. There is also the input USB-C port which the computer would be plugged into. This is so the other ports on the device can connect to the laptop. Additionally, there is the seven segment display to show the fan speed to the user.

The outputs are a four digit seven segment display, USB ports, fan motor, and LED strip. The number displayed on the seven segment dislay and actual fan motor speed will change depending on the selected fan speed. The LED strip will change mode/color through selection with two specific SPST switches.The USB ports will automatically work if a laptop is plugged into the USB-C input port.

Reassembly

Once the internal components were examined and photographed, the laptop fan was reassembled. The interactive buttons, display, and USB and power ports were tested and the laptop fan opperated as normal.

Lessons Learned

This project provided a hands-on understanding of embedded system integration in consumer electronics. Key takeaways included PCB trace routing, motor speed, and LED management.
